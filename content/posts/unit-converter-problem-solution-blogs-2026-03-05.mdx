## The Hidden Costs of Inaccurate Conversions

Even small errors in unit conversion can have serious consequences. Here’s why relying on manual tools or basic calculator tools is risky:

### 1. **Time Wasted on Corrections**
If you manually convert 2.5 kilometers to miles and miscalculate by 0.1 mile, fixing the error later costs time. Multiply this by daily tasks, and hours are lost to verification.

### 2. **Financial Losses**
In manufacturing or construction, a 1% error in material volume (e.g., concrete for a foundation) can lead to over-ordering or project delays. A study by the National Institute of Standards and Technology found such errors cost U.S. businesses over $300 million annually.

### 3. **Safety Risks**
In healthcare, a nurse might misread 5 mg vs. 5 mL for medication dosages. In engineering, incorrect torque measurements on bolts could compromise structural integrity.
