<h2>Why Strong Passwords Matter</h2>

Before diving into the *how*, let’s cover the *why*. Weak passwords—like “123456” or “password”—are cracked in seconds. Cybercriminals use brute-force attacks, dictionary attacks, and leaked databases to guess passwords. A strong password should:

- Be at least 12 characters long.
- Include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters.
- Combine numbers and symbols.
- Avoid common words or phrases.

Manually creating such passwords for every account is impractical. A **password generator** automates this process, ensuring each password is unique, complex, and unpredictable.

<h2>Best Practices for Password Creation</h2>

A **password generator** does the heavy lifting, but human oversight is still critical. Follow these guidelines:

- **Avoid reusing passwords** across accounts.
- **Store passwords securely** using a trusted password manager.
- **Update passwords regularly**, especially for sensitive accounts.
- **Enable 2FA (two-factor authentication)** for an extra layer of security.

For example, if you generate a password like `7tK!pQ9@wR4sL$`, pair it with 2FA to block unauthorized access even if the password is leaked.
