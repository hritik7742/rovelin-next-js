<h2>Why Manual Exports Fall Short</h2>

Most AI platforms don’t natively support exporting entire chat histories. Users often resort to manual workarounds, such as screenshots or copy-pasting text into documents. While these methods are technically free, they’re riddled with drawbacks:

- **Time-consuming**: Copy-pasting line-by-line is tedious, especially for long conversations.
- **Formatting loss**: Manual text pastes often strip formatting, making exported chats hard to read.
- **Media limitations**: Screenshots don’t include code blocks, tables, or other rich content.
- **Version control**: Saving multiple copies becomes disorganized without standardized naming.

For professionals managing 20+ chat sessions weekly, these inefficiencies add up. A single mistake—like missing a line or misnaming a file—can lead to lost data or wasted hours.
