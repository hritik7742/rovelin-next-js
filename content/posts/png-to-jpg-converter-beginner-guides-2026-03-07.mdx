## Why Convert PNG to JPG?

PNG and JPEG are two of the most common image formats, each with unique strengths. PNGs support transparency and lossless compression, making them ideal for logos, icons, and graphics with sharp edges. JPEGs, on the other hand, compress images with lossy methods, which means smaller file sizes at the cost of some quality. Here’s why you might prefer JPEG:

1. **Smaller File Sizes**: JPGs are typically 30–70% smaller than PNGs with similar visual quality.
2. **Wider Compatibility**: JPEG is supported by nearly all devices, web browsers, and email clients.
3. **Faster Loading**: Smaller files mean faster website and app performance—critical for user experience.
4. **Easier Sharing**: Platforms like Instagram, LinkedIn, and email services often require JPEG for optimal functionality.

However, converting PNG to JPG isn’t just about swapping extensions—it involves careful handling of transparency, color depth, and resolution. Let’s break down the process.

## Key Differences Between PNG and JPG

Understanding the differences between formats helps you avoid common pitfalls during conversion. Here’s a quick comparison:

| Feature                | PNG                            | JPEG                          |
|------------------------|--------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Transparency**       | Full support for alpha channel | No transparency support       |
| **Compression**        | Lossless                       | Lossy                         |
| **File Size**          | Larger than JPEG               | Smaller than PNG              |
| **Best For**           | Graphics, logos, text          | Photos, web imagery           |
| **Color Depth**        | 24-bit RGB + alpha             | 24-bit RGB                    |

*Note: Converting a PNG to JPG permanently removes transparency. Always plan accordingly.*
