## Mistake 1: Letting Custom Prompts Become Disorganized

### The Hidden Cost of Unmanaged Prompts

Custom prompts are the backbone of Claude ToolKit's efficiency. However, without a systematic approach, they quickly turn into a chaotic mess. Users often create prompts with vague names like “Marketing Idea 1” or “Project X,” making retrieval impossible after a few weeks.

**Example:** A content writer creates 50+ prompts for different client tones but never organizes them. When a deadline approaches, they waste hours searching for the right prompt instead of generating content.

### How to Structure Prompts for Scalability

1. **Adopt a naming convention** – Use `ClientName-ProjectType-Tone` (e.g., “HealthCorp-Email-Formal”)
2. **Tag prompts** with metadata like `SEO`, `UX`, or `Social Media`
3. **Group related prompts** into folders based on use cases (marketing, sales, technical documentation)

**Pro tip:** Use the search function to review and consolidate duplicate prompts monthly.

## Mistake 6: Not Customizing Prompts for Specific Use Cases

### Beyond Generic Prompts

Many users treat custom prompts like one-size-fits-all templates. But the most successful implementers tailor each prompt to specific scenarios with clear constraints.

**Before vs After:**  
❌ *Generic:* “Write a social media post”  
✅ *Specific:* “Create 3 Twitter threads for #TechMarketing trends [audience: SaaS startups] [max 280 chars] [add emoji]”

### Prompt Optimization Framework

1. **Define audience** and intent explicitly  
2. **Set hard constraints** (word count, format, tone)  
3. **Include examples** of desired output  
4. **Add negative constraints** (“Avoid technical jargon”)

**Test this method:** Take your 5 most used prompts and apply the optimization framework. Measure quality improvement.
