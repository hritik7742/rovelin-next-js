<h2>Why Compress Video Files?</h2>

Before we get into the how-to, let’s cover the why. Video compression solves three major pain points:

1. **Faster Uploads and Downloads**: Large videos take forever to transfer. Compression cuts file sizes, making sharing via email or cloud services more efficient.
2. **Storage Efficiency**: A 2-minute 4K video at 30fps can be over 2GB. Compression can reduce this to 300MB without noticeable quality loss.
3. **Compatibility**: Many platforms impose file size limits. For example, Instagram restricts videos over 2GB, and email providers often cap attachments at 25MB.

<h2>Best Practices for Video Optimization</h2>

Follow these tips to maximize the benefits of [Video Compressor](https://www.rovelin.com/tools/video-compressor):

### 1. **Batch Process Multiple Files**
If you’re compressing several videos (e.g., a day’s worth of project footage), run them individually in the same session. The tool allows you to queue multiple files for faster workflow.

### 2. **Check Output Quality**
After compression, preview the video on your device. Use high and low lighting conditions to ensure clarity isn’t compromised.

**Example**: A 1080p tutorial video compressed at 60% might look sharp on a smartphone but slightly softer on a 4K monitor. Adjust settings as needed.

### 3. **Use Compression for Platform-Specific Uploads**
Different platforms require different formats. For example:
- **YouTube**: Compress to 1080p MP4 for faster uploads.
- **LinkedIn**: Optimize to 720p for smoother playback on older devices.
- **TikTok**: Trim resolution to 1080x1920 pixels for vertical videos.

### 4. **Archive Originals Separately**
Always keep a copy of your original, uncompressed file. You can rework it if needed for future edits.
