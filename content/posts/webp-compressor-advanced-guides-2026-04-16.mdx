## How WebP Compression Algorithms Work (The Technical Deep Dive)

WebP uses the RICE (Range Encoder with Integer Cosine Transform) algorithm, combining predictive coding and entropy compression. Here’s a breakdown of the core mechanisms:

1. **Lossy Compression**: Removes subtle color and detail variations imperceptible to the human eye.
2. **Lossless Compression**: Retains every pixel, ideal for logos or diagrams.
3. **Alpha Channel Compression**: Optimizes transparency without bloating file size.

When using tools like [WebP Compressor](https://www.rovelin.com/tools/webp-compressor), the software locally applies these algorithms, ensuring no data is sent to external servers. This client-side approach guarantees privacy and compliance with data-sensitive workflows.
