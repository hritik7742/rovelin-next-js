<h2>1. Cooking and Baking: From Cups to Grams</h2>

<p>Cooking often involves recipe adjustments, but most recipes use inconsistent units. A muffin recipe might list eggs in pieces and flour in cups, while your kitchen scale measures grams. Converting these units manually increases the risk of errors that can ruin a dish.</p>

<ol>
  <li>**Recipe Scaling** – Doubling a cookie recipe requires converting 240g of butter to 1 cup if the original uses grams. A unit converter ensures accuracy without kitchen math.</li>
  <li>**International Recipes** – A French pastry recipe may specify 250 millilitres of milk, while your measuring cup is labeled in fluid ounces. Conversion tools bridge this gap instantly.</li>
  <li>**Ingredient Substitution** – Replacing granulated sugar with powdered sugar requires adjusting volume and weight—conversion tools provide exact ratios to maintain texture and sweetness.</li>
</ol>

<h2>2. Home Improvement Projects: Millimeters to Inches</h2>

<p>Home DIY projects demand precise measurements, but unit mismatches are common between furniture dimensions, tool markings, and materials. A unit converter saves time and avoids costly mistakes.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Furniture Assembly:</strong> A desk labeled at 122 cm might need conversion to inches (48 inches) for wall spacing or door clearance.</li>
  <li><strong>Painting Surfaces:</strong> Calculating paint volume for a 200 square foot wall requires converting square feet to square meters if using metric-based paint coverage charts.</li>
  <li><strong>Tile Installation:</strong> Converting 30 cm tiles to inches (11.81 inches) ensures accurate layout planning without overlapping tiles.</li>
</ul>

<h2>3. Personal Health and Fitness</h2>
