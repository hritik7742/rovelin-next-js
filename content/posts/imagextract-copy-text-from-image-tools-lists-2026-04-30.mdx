<h2>FAQ: Common Questions About OCR Tools</h2>

### <h3>1. Is OCR accurate for handwritten text?</h3>

Modern OCR tools like ABBYY FineReader and Adobe Scan support handwriting recognition. However, accuracy depends on legibility and language. For cursive or stylized handwriting, consider using tools with machine learning models trained on specific writing styles.

### <h3>2. Can I extract text from a video’s subtitle file?</h3>

Yes! Tools like OnlineOCR.net and Adobe Premiere Pro (with OCR integration) can analyze subtitle files. For browser-based workflows, ImageXtract focuses on static images and screenshots rather than video content.

### <h3>3. How secure is my data when using OCR tools?</h3>

**[ImageXtract - Copy Text from Image](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/enafhefnjpdnhbmccghnphjjlflohpkg)** processes data locally in your browser, minimizing privacy risks. For sensitive documents, avoid free online OCR services, as they may store your files indefinitely.

### <h3>4. What file types does OCR support?</h3>

Most OCR tools handle JPG, PNG, and PDF files. Advanced tools like ABBYY FineReader also support DOCX, XLSX, and PowerPoint for document conversion.

### <h3>5. Can OCR extract tables and diagrams?</h3>

Yes, but results vary. ABBYY FineReader and Adobe Scan excel at table recognition. For diagrams or charts, consider using AI tools like BoardReader or specialized data visualization software.
